## What is a General Power of Attorney?

A general power of attorney is a legal document that authorizes another person (called an "agent" or "attorney-in-fact") to act on your behalf in financial, legal, and personal matters.

**Key difference:** Unlike a limited power of attorney (which only works for specific situations), a general power gives your agent broad authority to act on your behalf.

## When Do You NEED a General Power of Attorney?

- **If you travel abroad** ‚Äî Your agent can manage your finances while you're away
- **If you have a business** ‚Äî Someone can sign documents on your behalf
- **If you consider aging or illness** ‚Äî Protect your assets if you become unable to act
- **If you own property** ‚Äî Facilitates real estate transactions
- **If you have bank accounts** ‚Äî Your agent can access and manage funds
- **If you're planning your future** ‚Äî Essential part of estate planning

## Types of Powers of Attorney in California

| Type | Duration | Best For |
|------|----------|---------|
| Durable General | Permanent (even if incapacitated) | Long-term planning |
| Temporary General | Specific (e.g., 6 months) | Temporary situations |

| Medical | Healthcare decisions | Medical directives |

## Step by Step: How to Create Your General Power of Attorney

### Step 1: Choose Your Agent
- Must be 18 years or older

### Step 2: Specify the Powers
